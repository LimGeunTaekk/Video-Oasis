Benchmark-wide pattern

High Benchmark Scores, High Shortcut Ratios

The shortcut examples above are not rare corner cases. Across 14 video benchmarks spanning spatial perception, temporal understanding, reasoning, and holistic video comprehension, we quantify how often each benchmark can be solved without full visual or temporal dependency. The results show that higher benchmark scores often coincide with higher shortcut solvability, suggesting inflated performance under weak video-native requirements.

Removing Shortcuts Reveals the Real Challenge

Once shortcut-solvable samples are removed, benchmark accuracy drops sharply across state-of-the-art video-LLMs. On the remaining video-native challenges, most models perform only marginally above random chance. This reveals that high benchmark scores can mask a substantial gap in genuine spatio-temporal understanding.

Diagnostic pipeline

What is Video-Oasis?

Video-Oasis turns the question “what should a video benchmark satisfy?” into a diagnostic process. It tests whether each sample truly depends on visual evidence, temporal order, and reliable annotation, rather than shortcut cues. Through this process, Video-Oasis filters out shortcut-solvable cases and distills the remaining samples into video-native challenges for genuine spatio-temporal understanding.

01 Test

Check visual evidence, temporal order, and annotation reliability.

02 Filter

Remove samples solvable through non-video shortcuts.

03 Distill

Reveal video-native spatio-temporal challenges.

Video-native challenges

What Challenges Remain After Filtering?

After filtering shortcut-solvable samples, V-Oasis exposes the video-native challenges that remain: tasks requiring fine-grained visual grounding, temporal continuity, causal interaction, and long-range narrative understanding.

01

Fine-Grained Perception

Ground subtle visual details in the surrounding video context.

02

Spatial World Understanding

Reason about objects, viewpoints, layouts, and spatial relations across scenes.

03

Temporal Dynamics & Tracking

Track actions, state changes, and object motion as events unfold over time.

04

Causal / Logical Reasoning

Connect events through causal, procedural, or multi-step reasoning.

05

Global Narrative

Integrate long-range evidence across multiple scenes into a coherent story.
